About

A global perspective on entrepreneurship.

Hana Milanov is a Professor of Entrepreneurship at IMD in Lausanne. With experience spanning five countries, she combines academic rigor, entrepreneurial insight, and a genuinely global perspective.

Her research — published in FT50 journals and featured in outlets including Forbes — examines how entrepreneurs recognize and develop opportunities, mobilize resources, and navigate the human dimensions of venture creation, including the role of identity, teams, and career context in shaping entrepreneurial decisions and outcomes.

Before joining IMD, she spent over a decade at the Technical University of Munich, where she served as Senior Vice-President for International Alliances and Alumni and as Academic Director of the FT #1-ranked EMBA in Innovation and Business Creation.

Research

Four threads in my work.

Opportunity & cognition

How entrepreneurs recognize, frame, and develop opportunities — and how mindset shapes what they pursue.

Resourcefulness under scarcity

Bricolage and resource mobilization when early-stage ventures have almost nothing to work with.

Gender, identity & inclusion

From femtech funding gaps to male allyship — how identity and bias shape who gets backed.

Networks, status & growth

How a venture’s first relationships imprint its future status, legitimacy, and path abroad.

Teaching & Executive Education

Participant-centered, across every level.

I teach across bachelor, master’s, MBA, Executive MBA, and doctoral programs — in five countries and in presential, online, and hybrid formats — using workshops, case-based teaching, role-plays, and lectures.

My goal is to orchestrate a participant-centered experience that develops critical and creative thinking and opens new perspectives on the material.
